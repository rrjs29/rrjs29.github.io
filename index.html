I am a postdoctoral researcher working with Prof. Justin C. Burton in the Deparment of Physics at Emory University. I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering and Applied Mechanics working with Prof. Paulo E. Arratia at the University of Pennsylvania in 2023.

My research focuses on characterizing the rheology (i.e., flow behaviors) of complex fluids, especially those containing particulates, polymers, and living organisms. From blood, mud, to cosmetic products, the presence of microstructures can impart nonlinear rheological properties that are not found in simple fluids like water. These unusual rheological behaviors, such as viscoelasticity and thixotropy, can give rise to instabilities found in industrial flow devices and anomalous transport in biological systems.
